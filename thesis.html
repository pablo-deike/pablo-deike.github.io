Closed-Form Distances and Divergences for Gaussian Mixtures in Probabilistic Machine Learning and Uncertainty Quantification

Abstract

Gaussian mixtures (GM) are basis functions for positive unit measures that, given enough regularity and a proper dissimilarity measure, are well suited for Probabilistic Machine Learning (PML) and Uncertainty Quantification (UQ).

The choice of distance or divergence used in both aspects affects computation and theoretical guarantees. In the PML aspect, during the Expectation Maximization (EM) algorithm used for fitting a GM to data, the stopping criterion usually checks convergence in the parameters rather than convergence in a distance or divergence. This is because the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence, a special case of the alpha-divergence and the f-divergence, does not admit a closed form. The Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) used during fitting is also based on the KL-divergence.

From the UQ aspect, the Bayesian update when a GM family is used on the prior and likelihood results in an increase in the number of components. A low-rank estimation to reduce the number of parameters based on a distance or divergence is therefore necessary.

The aim of the thesis is to use distances and divergences that admit a closed form for Gaussian mixtures to investigate and improve processes within both the PML and UQ aspects.
